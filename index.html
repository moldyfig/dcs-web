Featuring music from Burkina Faso, West Africa, performed in the epic historic Icehouse

Musicians

Arouna Diarra: n'goni, djembe, balafon, kora,
and talking drum
Yacouba Traore: djembe and djeli ngoni
Sonja Branch: doun doun and percussion
Rachel Mari Kimber: cello

Arouna Diarra, originally from Burkina Faso, West Africa, is an accomplished multi-instrumentalist born into a family of musicians, poets, and storytellers. His central instrument is the kamale n'goni, a 14-string harp-like instrument. Whether performing on stage at Red Rocks, NPR's Tiny Desk, the jungle of Costa Rica, or intimate community settings, Arouna brings joy and a connection to traditional folk music.
